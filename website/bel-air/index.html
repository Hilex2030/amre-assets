About Bel Air

A market with character.

Bel Air is three gates and three different markets. East Gate, with its Sunset-adjacent address and walkable-to-Hotel-Bel-Air convenience. West Gate, deeper into Stone Canyon, where lots widen, the foliage thickens, and value is increasingly defined by privacy. And the upper reaches climbing toward Mulholland Drive, where view and architectural significance carry the price tag. Knowing which gate, which canyon, and which view corridor changes everything.

AMRE Real Estate Group has direct transaction experience in Bel Air — most recently representing the seller of 14423 Mulholland Drive, a Bel Air ranch with sweeping San Fernando Valley views, sold for $1.72M (1.5% over asking) in just 8 days on market. Michael's architecture background is particularly relevant here, where the housing stock skews architecturally significant and where understanding renovation potential and structural integrity makes the difference between a great and merely adequate transaction.

What to know

Bel Air at a glance.

01

East Gate

Sunset-adjacent, walkable in the right pockets, a different kind of Bel Air. Period-revival estates and contemporary rebuilds dominate. Faster pace than the upper canyons.

02

Stone Canyon & West Gate

Deeper privacy, larger lots, longer drives. Landscape is the amenity. Many of the most consequential trades in LA happen here — quietly, often off-market.

03

Upper Bel Air & Mulholland Corridor

View-driven pricing. Our Mulholland Drive transaction lives here — see the case study below.

04

Architecturally significant inventory

Bel Air carries an unusually high concentration of design-led homes by named architects. Michael's licensed-architect background reads these properties differently than most agents.
