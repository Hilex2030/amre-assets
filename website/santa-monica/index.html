About Santa Monica

A market with character.

Santa Monica is where Los Angeles meets the ocean — and where the rules change. Rent control under the Costa-Hawkins framework, the California Coastal Commission's overlay, distinct local building codes, and a deep stock of duplexes and triplexes built before 1979 all shape what you can buy, sell, and develop here. Beneath all of that, the four primary sub-markets — North of Montana, Sunset Park, Ocean Park, and Mid-City — each have their own lifestyle, price profile, and buyer type.

AMRE Real Estate Group represents both buyers and sellers across Santa Monica, with particular strength in the multi-unit and architecturally significant residential segments. Michael's architect background and the team's familiarity with coastal compliance, tenant estoppels, and rent-board protocols make us a meaningful partner whether you're acquiring a duplex on Ocean Park, selling a North-of-Montana single-family, or navigating a 1031 exchange into a Santa Monica income property.

What to know

Santa Monica at a glance.

01

North of Montana

Single-family premium-priced homes north of Montana Avenue. School-district demand and walkability to the village core drive pricing. Period homes and contemporary rebuilds in roughly equal measure.

02

Ocean Park & Sunset Park

Beach-adjacent, character-rich neighborhoods with a high density of duplexes, triplexes, and architecturally significant single-family homes. Owner-occupant and small-investor crossover.

03

Multi-family & rent-control

Pre-1979 buildings carry rent-control implications. Estoppel certificates, tenant histories, and local rent-board compliance matter. We've represented investors in this segment.

04

Coastal Commission overlay

Properties west of Lincoln Boulevard carry coastal-zone considerations. Permitting, additions, and short-term-rental rules differ from the rest of LA.
